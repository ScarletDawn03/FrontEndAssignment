State of Kedah

Kedah, in northwest Malaysia, is renowned for its lush rice fields and agricultural heritage. Known as the "Rice Bowl" of Malaysia, it features the scenic Langkawi Archipelago with its beautiful beaches and tropical landscapes. The state also offers historical sites and vibrant markets, blending natural beauty with cultural significance.

Langkawi Sky Bridge

The Langkawi Sky Bridge is a remarkable 125-meter-long pedestrian bridge located on Langkawi Island, Malaysia, suspended 700 meters above sea level at the peak of Gunung Mat Cincang. Accessible via the Langkawi Cable Car, the bridge features a distinctive curved design supported by a single pylon and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding rainforest, Andaman Sea, and neighboring islands. Its transparent floor sections provide an exhilarating experience and an unobstructed view of the lush landscape below, making it a popular and impressive tourist attraction.

Cenang Beach

Cenang Beach is a vibrant and popular stretch of coastline on Langkawi Island, Malaysia, known for its lively atmosphere and beautiful sandy shores. The beach offers a picturesque setting with its clear turquoise waters and soft, white sand, ideal for sunbathing and swimming. Lined with numerous restaurants, bars, and shops, Cenang Beach provides ample dining and entertainment options. The area is also renowned for its water sports, including jet skiing, parasailing, and banana boat rides, making it a lively hub for both relaxation and adventure.

Eagle Square

Eagle Square, located in the heart of Abuja, Nigeria's capital, is a striking public space renowned for its grandiose design and monumental sculptures. The square features an impressive display of two giant eagle statues, symbolizing strength and unity, perched on large pedestals that face the entrance. The surrounding area is beautifully landscaped, with wide, open spaces and well-maintained gardens, offering a scenic and peaceful environment. Eagle Square is often used for official ceremonies, national celebrations, and public events, reflecting the nation's pride and cultural heritage.

Wat Lampam

Wat Lampam, also known as the White Temple, is located in Alor Setar, Kedah. It is a beautifully intricate Buddhist temple that features several meticulously maintained buildings. The temple is significant to the local Siamese population and provides a serene environment for visitors, who are required to remove their shoes before entering. The temple grounds offer an opportunity to interact with animals and enjoy the peaceful surroundings. Entry to Wat Lampam is free, making it an accessible and inviting destination for those exploring the cultural and religious sites in Kedah.

Alor Setar Tower

The Alor Setar Tower, located in Alor Setar, Kedah, Malaysia, is a striking telecommunications and observation tower renowned for its modern design and panoramic views. Standing at 165.5 meters, it is the tallest structure in Malaysia's northern region. The tower features a sleek, futuristic appearance with a glass-paneled observation deck that offers sweeping vistas of the surrounding landscape, including the Andaman Sea on clear days. In addition to its primary function as a communications hub, the Alor Setar Tower also houses a revolving restaurant and provides visitors with a unique vantage point to experience the region's natural beauty and urban landscape.
