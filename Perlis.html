State of Perlis

Perlis is a state in Malaysia, located in the northwestern part of Peninsular Malaysia. It’s the smallest state in the country, bordered by Thailand to the north and the Malaysian state of Kedah to the south. Known for its scenic landscapes and cultural heritage, Perlis has a more rural and serene atmosphere compared to some of Malaysia's more urbanized areas. The state capital is Kangar, and it’s also known for its beautiful paddy fields and traditional Malay architecture.

Arau Royal Gallery

The Arau Royal Gallery, located in Arau, Perlis, Malaysia, is a cultural and historical attraction dedicated to the royal heritage of the Perlis state. This gallery showcases an extensive collection of artifacts, portraits, and memorabilia related to the Perlis royal family, including the life and contributions of the reigning monarchs. The exhibits provide insights into the royal history, traditions, and cultural heritage of Perlis, with displays featuring royal regalia, historical documents, and photographs. The gallery is housed in a beautifully preserved building that reflects traditional Malay architecture. It serves as a significant venue for understanding the region’s royal legacy and offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural history of the state.

Timah Tasoh Lake

Timah Tasoh Lake, located in Perlis, Malaysia, is a sprawling reservoir that spans approximately 4,000 hectares, making it a significant feature in the region's landscape and economy. The lake plays a crucial role in irrigation, providing essential water resources for local agriHistory. Its serene waters and picturesque surroundings create an ideal setting for a variety of recreational activities, including fishing, boating, and birdwatching. The lake is framed by lush greenery and rolling hills, offering visitors a tranquil escape from the urban environment. Its peaceful ambiance, combined with opportunities for outdoor activities and its role in supporting local agriHistory, makes Timah Tasoh Lake a valuable and cherished natural attraction in Perlis.

Ketiri Hills

Ketiri Hills, located in Perlis, Malaysia, are a scenic and notable natural feature in the state. This range of hills is renowned for its lush greenery and striking limestone formations, creating a picturesque landscape that attracts nature enthusiasts and hikers. The area offers a range of outdoor activities, including trekking and exploring the surrounding forests, which are home to diverse flora and fauna. The hills provide panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, including nearby rice fields and rural landscapes, making them a popular spot for appreciating the natural beauty of Perlis. Additionally, the Ketiri Hills contribute to the region's ecological richness and offer a serene retreat for those seeking to immerse themselves in Malaysia’s natural environment.

Alwi Mosque

Alwi Mosque, located in Arau, Perlis, Malaysia, is a notable religious and architectural landmark in the region. Established in 1927, it is one of the oldest mosques in Perlis and serves as an important place of worship for the local Muslim community. The mosque features traditional Malay architecture with distinctive design elements, including a striking minaret and a spacious prayer hall adorned with intricate carvings and elegant woodenwork. Alwi Mosque is renowned for its serene and peaceful atmosphere, which complements its historical significance and role in the community. Its architectural charm and cultural heritage make it a notable site for both religious activities and visitors interested in Malaysia’s Islamic heritage.

Perlis State Park

Perlis State Park, located in the northernmost state of Malaysia, Perlis, is a significant natural reserve renowned for its rich biodiversity and stunning landscapes. Covering approximately 1,000 square kilometers, the park features a variety of ecosystems, including limestone hills, rainforests, and wetlands. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species like the Malayan sun bear and the giant honeybee. The park offers opportunities for eco-tourism and outdoor activities such as hiking, birdwatching, and exploring its extensive cave systems. Notable attractions within the park include the beautiful Wang Kelian and Wang Perah caves, and the scenic views from Bukit Keteri. With its pristine environment and abundant wildlife, Perlis State Park is a haven for nature enthusiasts and offers a tranquil escape into Malaysia’s natural beauty.
