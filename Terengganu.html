State of Terengganu

Terengganu, on Malaysia's east coast, is known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and picturesque islands like Redang. The state boasts rich cultural heritage, with traditional Malay architecture, intricate crafts like batik, and distinctive cuisine such as nasi dagang. This combination of natural beauty and cultural depth makes Terengganu a captivating destination.

Kuala Terengganu Drawbridge

The Kuala Terengganu Drawbridge is Malaysia's first drawbridge, connecting Kuala Nerus and Kuala Terengganu across the Terengganu River. Completed in 2019, it is part of the East Coast Economic Region projects, costing RM 248 million. Inspired by London's Tower Bridge, it features four 15-storey towers and a skybridge. The bridge facilitates faster travel between key locations and is a prominent feature of the Kuala Terengganu City Council's development. It officially opened on August 1, 2019.

Perhentian Island

The Perhentian Islands are a group of small islands in Malaysia, off the coast of Terengganu. They are known for their beautiful beaches, clear waters, and diverse marine life, making them popular for snorkeling, diving, and relaxation. The two main islands are Perhentian Besar and Perhentian Kecil, catering to different types of travelers. The islands are part of a marine park, which helps protect the coral reefs and marine ecosystem. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from March to October.

Redang Island

Redang Island is a Malaysian island in the South China Sea, known for its clear waters, white sandy beaches, and rich marine life. It is a popular destination for snorkeling, diving, and other water activities. The island features several resorts and is part of the Redang Marine Park, which helps protect its natural environment. Accessibility is mainly by boat, with regular ferry services from the mainland. Conservation efforts are in place to preserve the island's biodiversity and natural beauty.

Chinatown

Chinatown in Kuala Terengganu, known locally as Kampung Cina, is a historical and cultural hub. Established in the late 19th century, it features traditional Chinese shophouses, temples, and clan associations. The area is a vibrant mix of Chinese heritage and local Malay History, attracting tourists for its architecture, street art, and food. Notable landmarks include the Ho Ann Kiong Temple and the Chinatown Heritage Walk, showcasing murals and informative plaques.

Crystal Mosque

The Crystal Mosque, or Masjid Kristal, is located in Kuala Terengganu, Malaysia, on Wan Man Island within the Islamic Heritage Park. Constructed from steel, glass, and crystal, it was completed in 2008. The mosque can accommodate over 1,500 worshipers and features nine large domes and 51 smaller domes, along with four minarets. It is known for its unique and modern architectural design.
