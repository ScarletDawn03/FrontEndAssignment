Perlis

Introduction

Perlis, the smallest state in Malaysia, is located in the northern part of the country, bordering Thailand to the north. Despite its size, Perlis is rich in natural beauty and cultural heritage. The state is known for its serene landscapes, including rolling paddy fields, limestone hills, and tranquil lakes. Key attractions include the picturesque Timah Tasoh Lake, the ancient limestone caves of Gua Kelam and Wang Burma, and the Perlis State Park, which offers a sanctuary for diverse flora and fauna.

1. Arau Royal Gallery

The Royal Gallery of Perlis, also known as Galeria Diraja Tuanku Syed Putra or Arau Royal Gallery, is located in Arau, the royal town of Perlis, Malaysia. This gallery is dedicated to showcasing the history, heritage, and legacy of the Perlis royal family, particularly focusing on Tuanku Syed Putra, the third Raja of Perlis and the longest-serving king of Perlis.

2.Gua Kelam

Kelam Cave, or Gua Kelam, is one of the most famous and intriguing natural attractions in Perlis, Malaysia. Located near the small town of Kaki Bukit, Gua Kelam is a limestone cave that offers a unique experience for visitors who are interested in nature, history, and adventure.

3.Wang Burma Cave

Wang Burma Cave is one of the hidden gems in Perlis, Malaysia, located within the Perlis State Park (Taman Negeri Perlis). It is known for its stunning limestone formations and offers a more adventurous caving experience compared to other caves in the region.

4.Timah Tasoh Lake

Timah Tasoh Lake is a picturesque and serene man-made reservoir located in the northern state of Perlis, Malaysia. The lake is formed by the confluence of the Timah and Tasoh rivers, which give it its name. It is not only a key water resource for the region but also a popular destination for nature lovers, bird watchers, and those seeking tranquility.

5.Ketiri Hills

Ketiri Hills, also known as Bukit Ketiri, is a lesser-known but beautiful hilly area located in Perlis, Malaysia. This natural attraction is part of the state's rich landscape of limestone hills and is popular among hikers, nature lovers, and those seeking outdoor adventure.

6. Alwi Mosque

Masjid Alwi, also known as Alwi Mosque, is one of the most prominent and historic mosques in Perlis, Malaysia. Located in the state capital, Kangar, the mosque is an important religious and cultural landmark for the local Muslim community and visitors alike.

7.The Perlis Vineyard

The Perlis Vineyard, also known as Taman Anggur Perlis or Perlis Grape Garden, is a unique and popular agricultural attraction located in the northernmost state of Perlis, Malaysia. The vineyard offers visitors a rare opportunity to experience grape cultivation in a tropical climate, which is not commonly associated with grape growing.

8.Tuanku Lailatul Shahreen Bougainvillea Garden

The Tuanku Lailatul Shahreen Bougainvillea Garden is a beautiful and vibrant botanical garden located in Perlis, Malaysia. Named after the Raja Perempuan of Perlis, Tuanku Lailatul Shahreen Akashah, the garden is renowned for its extensive collection of bougainvillea plants, offering a colorful and serene environment for visitors.

9.Perlis State Park

Perlis State Park is a significant natural reserve located in the northernmost state of Perlis, Malaysia. It spans the western part of the Nakawan Range, which is the longest continuous limestone hill in the country. The park is known for its rich biodiversity, unique limestone karst formations, and extensive cave systems. It is a haven for nature lovers, hikers, and those interested in the flora and fauna of Malaysia.
